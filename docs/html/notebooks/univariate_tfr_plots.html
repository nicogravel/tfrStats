Plot TFRs¶

In the present illustration, we exhibit Time-Frequency Representations (TFRs) that are enhanced with statistical delineation based on p-value computations (specifically, p ≤ 0.05 denotes significance), as depicted by the blue trace in the upper panel. Concomitantly, an alpha mask demarcates regions surpassing the established null percentile threshold—the 95th percentile, to be precise. This partitioning is visually articulated by the red trace in the lower panel, which delineates the corresponding p-value distribution.

As we analyze spectral power across the dimensions of frequency, spatial distribution, and temporal evolution, it is imperative to address the aggregation of data within pre-determined frequency bands and spatial regions, such as Regions of Interest (ROIs). Such an approach necessitates a slight modification to the process of multiple comparisons correction. This refinement ensures the robustness and validity of statistical inferences drawn from spatial TFR data, considering the intricacies inherent to the multidimensional dataset at hand.

[For further study on the implications of multiple comparisons in neuroimaging, refer to the seminal paper by Bennett, Wolford, and Miller (2009) in ‘NeuroImage’, titled “The principled control of false positives in neuroimaging”.]
